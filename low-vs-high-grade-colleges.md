# 💡 Why fees are so low in colleges like Besant Women College

## 1. Government-aided institution

Many older colleges in Mangalore/Udupi are **“government-aided”**.

👉 That means:

* Government pays a large part of **teacher salaries**
* Infrastructure may be partly funded or supported
* College does **not need to recover full cost from students**

So students pay only a **small portion of actual cost**.

---

## 2. Affiliation with a public university

Colleges like this are affiliated to Mangalore University.

👉 This leads to:

* **Standardized syllabus**
* **Controlled fee structure**
* No “premium pricing” like private universities

---

## 3. Non-profit / trust-based management

Institutions like Besant are usually run by **educational trusts or societies**, not profit-driven companies.

👉 Their goal is:

* Provide **affordable education**
* Support **local students**, especially girls

---

## 4. Basic infrastructure (not luxury campus)

Let’s be practical:

* Labs are **functional, not high-end**
* No heavy investment in **advanced hospital simulation**
* Fewer “premium” facilities compared to Manipal/Yenepoya

👉 Lower investment = lower fees

---

## 5. Limited clinical exposure compared to premium institutes

Unlike Manipal Academy of Higher Education or Yenepoya University:

* No large **attached super-speciality hospital**
* Less exposure to advanced clinical cases

👉 So the training is more **academic + basic practical**

---

## 6. Subsidized courses like Home Science / Nutrition

Courses like **Food & Nutrition / Home Science** are traditionally:

* Less equipment-heavy than Radiology or OT Technology
* Supported by government schemes for women education

👉 Naturally lower fees

---

# ⚖️ Simple Comparison

| Factor            | Govt-aided College | Private University |
| ----------------- | ------------------ | ------------------ |
| Fees              | ₹5k–₹20k/year      | ₹1–2 lakh/year     |
| Infrastructure    | Basic              | Advanced           |
| Clinical exposure | Moderate           | High               |
| Brand value       | Moderate           | High               |

---

# ✅ Is low fee a disadvantage?

Not necessarily. It depends on your goal:

### 👍 Good choice if:

* Budget is limited
* You plan to do **MSc later**
* You want to build skills independently

### ⚠️ Consider higher-end colleges if:

* You want **hospital-based clinical dietitian role immediately**
* You prefer **campus placements + exposure**

---

# 🎯 Smart strategy (recommended)

A very practical path:

1. Do **low-cost BSc (Besant / similar college)**
2. Then do **MSc Nutrition from a better institute**
3. Get **Registered Dietitian certification**
4. Build career / freelance practice

👉 This saves money **without compromising career growth**
